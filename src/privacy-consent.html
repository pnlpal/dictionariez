Privacy Notice

Your privacy is our priority. Dictionariez is a freemium extension, and how your data is handled depends on your usage:

  • Free Users: Your word lookup history and settings are stored locally in your browser using its built-in storage and sync features. We do not collect or transmit your data to our servers, nor do we share it with third parties.
  • TTS Trial (Free Users): Free users can trial our Text-to-Speech (TTS) service with limited usage. When using this feature, the selected text is temporarily sent to our servers to generate speech audio. Text content is processed in real-time and not permanently stored after processing.
  • Pro Users (Cloud Sync & TTS): If you subscribe to the Pro plan, your lookup history and settings will be securely stored on our cloud servers to enable cross-device sync. Pro users also get unlimited TTS usage, which sends selected text to our servers for speech generation. For these services, the extension uses cookies to keep you logged in.
  • Clipboard Access: If you enable the "read clipboard" feature, the extension will access clipboard content for word lookups, but this data is not sent anywhere unless you use cloud sync or TTS features.
  • Tab URL: If you use the "watch-on-captionz" button on YouTube, the extension will access the current tab's URL and send it to the Captionz server for processing. You can disable this feature in the settings.

Summary: For free users, your data stays on your device except when using TTS trial (text sent for speech generation). For Pro users, lookup history, settings, and TTS text are processed/synced via our cloud servers. You can disable TTS and clear your data at any time in the extension settings.

Read more at our Mozilla Add-on privacy page.
